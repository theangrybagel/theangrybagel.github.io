Combat Rules

Dealing Damage

Calculating Base Damage

To calculate base damage, add the weapons damage value and modifiers associated to that weapon type. If there are multiple types for a weapon, add all of the modifiers for each of the types (discard duplicate modififiers). If the target has armor, subtract the total defense of their armor from the base damage.

Weapon Type Modifiers (skills)
M + Melee + Beef/3
R + Dexterity/3
S + Small Weapons
L + (Beef/3 + Large Weapons)/2

Calculating Hits/Misses

To calculate whether or not an action misses, consult the following instructions:
If the range of the weapon is less than the distance between the attacker and the target, the attack misses. Otherwise, continue. Roll a D20 and add the attackers dexterity stat to that, and then roll a D20 and add the targets agility stat (as well as their dodging skill) to that. If the attacker has a higher number than the target, the attack hits. If it's a tie, the defender takes only a fourth of the damage. If the attacker rolls lower than the target, the attack misses.


Calculating Effects

The following is a table of weapon effects and what they do. Some effects are followed by a number, this number is represented as p

Effect Name Instructions
Razor If D6 < p, ignore any defenses lower than level 1.
Bleed If D6 < p, and body damage is done (as in damage to the target and just the armor), and the damage is greater than 3, the target now bleeds for p amount of turns, losing D6 health per turn.
Burn Deals burning damage to the target and its armor. Attacks to explosives triggerable by heat can cause explosions.
Peirce If D6 > 4, and p >= the targets armor level, deal base damage to armor and base damage divided by 4 to the target.

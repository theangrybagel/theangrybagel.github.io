Misc (RPG Mechanics)

Rolling

Custom rolls in this guide are represented with R and another number. R15, R9, R32, and R300 are all examples of custom rolls. Other rolls will be represented with a number (optional) and a type of die. Examples of these are D10, D20, 2D6, 3D20.

Regular Dice

To roll for something that uses a regular dice, such as a D20, or a 2D6, simply get out that kind of die and roll it. If there is a number in front of the dice indicator, such as 2D6, roll multiple times (as many times as the number shows) and add each roll to get your final result.

Custom Rolls

Custom rolls, represented with R along with another number, are used mostly when calculating things like damage. To get the value for a custom roll, roll 2D10s to get a value from 1-100. Next, divide that value by 100. Finally, multiply it by the number after the R. If the number after the R is 23, and we roll 74, the final value is (74/100) * 23, which is about 17. Be sure to round to the nearest integer.
